## Proposed New Variants

## Tier 1: High Priority (Next 12 Months)

### 3. SCHOLAR Scale™ (Academic/Research)
**Full Name:** Scholarly Content and Hypothesis Assessment for Laboratory & Applied Research  
**Target:** Academic papers, research reports, preprints, dissertations  

**Proposed Criteria (6):**
- **MR** - Methodology Rigor (0-100)
  - Experimental design quality
  - Sample size/power analysis
  - Controls and variables documented
  - Reproducibility score
  
- **SR** - Statistical Rigor (0-100)
  - Appropriate statistical tests
  - P-value interpretation
  - Effect size reporting
  - Multiple comparison corrections
  
- **PR** - Peer Review Status (0-100)
  - Publication venue quality (impact factor)
  - Review process transparency
  - Post-publication peer commentary
  - Preprint vs. published status
  
- **CQ** - Citation Quality (0-100)
  - Primary vs. secondary sources
  - Citation recency and relevance
  - Self-citation rate
  - Retracted citation detection
  
- **DT** - Data Transparency (0-100)
  - Data availability statement
  - Code sharing (GitHub/OSF)
  - Materials sharing
  - Preregistration status
  
- **CI** - Conflict of Interest Disclosure (0-100)
  - Funding source disclosure
  - Industry relationships
  - Author conflicts documented
  - Financial ties transparency

**Grade Labels:**
- A+/A (90-100): Publication-ready, reproducible
- B (75-89): Minor revisions needed
- C (60-74): Major revisions required
- D (45-59): Reject with resubmit option
- F (0-44): Fundamental flaws, reject

**Market Opportunity:**
- Universities (research integrity offices)
- Funding agencies (NIH, NSF, etc.)
- Publishers/journals (desk rejection automation)
- Tenure committees
- **TAM:** $500M-1.5B (research integrity market)

**Unique Features:**
- Detect paper mill content
- Flag image manipulation
- Identify AI-generated sections in methods/results
- Compare to Retraction Watch database

## Implementation Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
1. Develop SCHOLAR Scale™ criteria framework
2. Create academic test dataset (50 papers: 10 excellent, 20 good, 10 mediocre, 10 poor)
3. Build academic-specific detectors:
   - Statistical test validator
   - Citation quality checker
   - P-hacking detector
   - Image manipulation scanner

### Phase 2: Build & Test (Months 4-6)
1. Implement SCHOLAR variant in codebase
2. Test on academic paper corpus
3. Validate with domain experts (professors, journal editors)
4. Iterate based on feedback

### Phase 3: Market Launch (Months 7-9)
1. Create academic-focused marketing materials
2. Partner with 3-5 universities for pilot
3. Present at academic conferences
4. Publish validation study

### Phase 4: Scale (Months 10-12)
1. Onboard paying customers
2. Develop API for journal integration
3. Build partnerships with publishers
4. Launch CORPORATE Scale™ (repeat process)

## Competitive Analysis

### Academic Integrity Market
**Competitors:** Turnitin, iThenticate, Grammarly (plagiarism only)  
**Gap:** None offer comprehensive research quality scoring  
**Advantage:** End-to-end quality assessment, not just plagiarism

### Corporate Analytics Market
**Competitors:** Bloomberg Terminal, S&P Capital IQ, FactSet  
**Gap:** Focus on data aggregation, not report quality assessment  
**Advantage:** Automated quality scoring, ESG greenwashing detection

### Legal Analytics Market
**Competitors:** ROSS Intelligence, CaseText, Ravel Law  
**Gap:** Focus on research, not document quality assessment  
**Advantage:** Automated brief quality scoring, AI disclosure tracking
